A complete step-by-step guide to your orthodontic care

🦷
We've prepared this guide so your treatment goes as smoothly as possible. Please read each step carefully and feel free to contact us anytime with questions — we're always available by text or email. 😊

Follow these steps in order

10 Steps to Your Perfect Smile

1
Review & Sign the Contract 📄
Online

We'll send you a link to your contract covering all details about your treatment, financial arrangements, and office policies. Take your time reading everything thoroughly.

  • Includes treatment details, financial terms, and office policies
  • Contact us with any questions before signing
2
Choose Your Payment Option 💳
Online or Text/Phone

Once you've signed, it's time to settle payment. Two options are available:

  • Full Payment — Any payment method accepted for the total amount
  • Payment Plan — Down payment required (any payment method)
    • Monthly payments via ACH transfer from checking account only
    • Credit cards cannot be used for monthly payments
3
Designing Your Treatment Plan 🧑‍⚕️
Doctor Review

After receiving your signed contract, we'll begin designing your personalized treatment plan.

  • Both doctors will review your case in detail
  • The best possible plan will be discussed and tailored specifically for you
4
Dental Clearance 🦷✅
Pre-Treatment

Before starting treatment, we want to ensure your teeth are in the best possible health.

  • Fill any cavities prior to starting
  • Complete any necessary cleanings
  • If you haven't seen a dentist in the last 6 months, we recommend doing so before moving forward
5
Records Appointment 📸
In-Person Visit

A follow-up appointment to update your records with the latest imaging.

  • Additional photos of your teeth will be taken
  • 3D X-ray (CBCT) to assess the health of your teeth, roots, and jawbone
  • If you have dental insurance, we'll submit the CBCT claim on your behalf
    • Reimbursement is not guaranteed but won't affect your treatment fee
  • If dental work has changed since your initial consultation, an additional 3D scan may be taken for accuracy
6
Manufacturing Your Appliance 🛠️
Lab Fabrication

After your records appointment, we carefully craft your custom appliance to suit your unique needs.

Typically 4–6 weeks to manufacture
7
Extractions — Lightforce or Metal Braces 🔧
If Required

For patients who require tooth extractions before metal braces or Lightforce placement:

  • Wait for our team to finalize your treatment plan before scheduling extractions
  • We'll notify you once we send the extraction referral letter to your dentist
  • Plan to complete the extraction 1–2 weeks before your braces placement appointment
8
Bonding Visit — Appliance Placement 💎
In-Person · 60 min

The big day! Your appliance is placed and you'll be set up for success.

  • We'll fit your appliance and ensure it's comfortable
  • Receive instruction on how to clean and care for your appliance
  • All your questions and any discomfort will be addressed
Please arrive on time — this appointment uses the full 60 minutes. If rescheduling is needed, notify us at least 48 hours in advance. Custom materials are prepared specifically for your scheduled date and cannot be reused if unused, which may result in additional costs.
9
Extractions — Invisalign Patients 🔧
If Required

For Invisalign patients who require extractions, the timing is different from braces patients:

  • Schedule your extraction after your Invisalign Bonding Visit
  • Aim to complete the extraction within 4 weeks after bonding day
This is the reverse order from metal/Lightforce braces patients — extractions happen after bonding for Invisalign.
10
Insurance Submission 🏦
We Handle It

We manage your insurance submission from start to finish. Here's what to keep in mind:

Coverage Requirement

Maintain coverage throughout your treatment to receive the full benefit amount.

Processing Time

Claims typically take 4–8 weeks to process depending on your carrier.

About Your EOB

The Explanation of Benefits amount is not what you owe — it shows only how insurance contributed.

Payout Schedule

Most insurers pay in monthly, quarterly, or annual installments, not a lump sum.

If you have multiple insurance plans, we'll calculate total benefits at the end of treatment and issue a check for any excess credits.
